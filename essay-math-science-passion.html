Original summary and analysis (non-copyrighted)

Summary: Persistence in Mathematics

Summary (original): The narrator describes a long-standing love of math and science, contrasting it with less exciting subjects. A childhood question about the usefulness of math becomes a turning point once calculus reveals real-world applications.

The essay shows that math is creative problem solving, not rote work. The writer highlights how proofs and classic ideas make difficult tasks simpler and more elegant.

Despite early struggles and being placed in a lower-level class, the student takes ownership: finding help, studying hard, and choosing a more challenging calculus path. The effort leads to success and reinforces persistence as a core trait.
