Ivy League “Minimum Requirements” (Important)

There are no official minimum GPA or test scores for Ivy League schools. Each uses holistic review, and policies change yearly. The safest approach is to meet all required application components and present the strongest possible academic record.

Always confirm current requirements on each university’s official admissions website.

What Top Universities Ask For

  • Academic rigor: challenging courses and strong grades.
  • Standardized tests: if required, show readiness (policies vary by year).
  • Strong essays: clear motivation, growth, and fit.
  • Recommendations: teachers who can speak to your work and character.
  • Activities: depth, leadership, and impact over quantity.
  • Projects or research: proof of curiosity and initiative.
  • Community impact: service or contribution to others.

Main Goals of Your Application

  • Show growth: how you improved or overcame challenges.
  • Show fit: why the university is right for you.
  • Show impact: what you changed or built.
  • Show direction: what you want to study and why.
  • Show character: responsibility, curiosity, resilience.

How a Strong CV Should Look

  • One page (most students): clean, simple layout.
  • Clear sections: Education, Activities, Awards, Projects, Skills.
  • Action verbs: Led, built, researched, organized.
  • Quantified impact: numbers, hours, results.
  • Consistency: dates, formatting, and grammar.

What Ivy League Schools Typically Look For

Holistic review: academics + character + impact + fit.
  • Academic excellence: top grades in the hardest courses you can take.
  • Depth over breadth: sustained commitment in 1–3 activities.
  • Leadership & initiative: start or lead something meaningful.
  • Distinctive strength: a clear area where you stand out (research, Olympiads, art, entrepreneurship, service).
  • Strong recommendations: teachers who know your work deeply.
  • Compelling essays: authentic voice, growth, and intellectual curiosity.
  • Context awareness: achievements understood in your school and country context.

What You Should Focus On

☑ 1 clear academic direction (major interest + reasons)
☑ 1 signature project or research experience
☑ 1 leadership role with measurable impact
☑ Evidence of intellectual curiosity outside class
☑ Strong teacher relationships (for recommendations)
☑ Essays that show identity, growth, and purpose
☑ A clean, honest CV with results and dates

Recommended CV Structure

Header: Name, email, phone, city, LinkedIn (optional)
Education: School, GPA (if strong), graduation date, top courses
Activities & Leadership: Role, organization, impact, dates
Awards & Honors: Scholarships, competitions, recognitions
Projects & Research: Title, tools used, results
Skills: Programming, lab tools, languages

Honors vs Medals vs Awards (What’s the Difference?)

  • Honors: Recognition for achievement or ranking (e.g., “Top 5% National Exam,” “Honor Roll,” “Dean’s List”).
  • Medals: Awards tied to competitions or contests (e.g., “Gold Medal — National Science Fair”).
  • Awards: Broad term that includes honors, medals, scholarships, or special recognitions.
Tip: Always add level + rank + year (e.g., “Silver Medal — Regional Math Olympiad, 2024 (Top 3/180)”).

Examples: Honors

  • Honor Roll (Grades 9–11)
  • Top 10% in National Exams
  • School Excellence Certificate

Examples: Medals

  • Gold Medal — Regional Science Fair
  • Silver Medal — Math Olympiad
  • Bronze Medal — Robotics Competition

Examples: Awards

  • Research Scholarship Winner
  • Best Project Award
  • Leadership Recognition

☑ 1–2 deep activities with leadership
☑ Essays with a clear personal story
☑ Strong recommendations (2–3 teachers)
☑ Projects or research that show initiative
☑ Clear academic direction or major interest
☑ Proof of impact (numbers, outcomes)
☑ Clean, concise CV

Quick Writing Tips for Top Universities

  • Write like a human, not a brochure.
  • Use specific examples, not general claims.
  • Connect each activity to learning or impact.
  • Explain why your goals make sense for your background.
  • Keep sentences clear and focused.
