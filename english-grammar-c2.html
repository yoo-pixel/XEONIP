C2 Grammar: Near-Native Mastery

C2 focuses on precision, register control, and high-level academic style.

You will learn how to write concisely, manage emphasis, and sustain clear argumentation.

These topics help you sound natural, formal, and persuasive when needed.

Chapter 1: Rhetorical control

Shape emphasis and argument flow with advanced structures.

  • Ellipsis & substitution for elegant repetition control
  • Fronting and inversion for emphasis
  • Theme-rheme control across paragraphs
  • It-clefts and wh-clefts for focus
  • Contrast pairs for persuasion (not only… but also…)

Chapter 3: Precision & nuance

Choose words and structures that match evidence strength.

  • Modality and stance (may, tends to, is likely to)
  • Controlled figurative language (minimal, precise)
  • Subtle distinctions: imply vs infer, affect vs effect
  • Strong vs weak claims in academic tone
  • Exact quantifiers and limits

Chapter 4: Cohesion at scale

Maintain coherence across long sections and arguments.

  • Macro-structure: claim → evidence → synthesis
  • Paragraph openings that signal direction
  • Advanced referencing (such, former/latter)
  • Consistent key terms across sections
  • Strategic repetition for clarity

Master punctuation and sentence control.

  • Semicolons, colons, and dashes for logic
  • Balanced parallel structures
  • Rhythm and cadence in academic style
  • Editing for precision without losing voice
  • Consistency in tense and perspective

Common C2 Errors to Avoid

❌ Overusing fancy vocabulary without precision.

✅ Choose exact words, not just complex ones.

❌ Mixing academic tone with informal idioms.

✅ Keep register consistent throughout.

❌ Writing long sentences with unclear structure.

✅ Use punctuation to show relationships clearly.
