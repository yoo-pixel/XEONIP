Key Punctuation Takeaways

‚úì Periods & Questions

Every sentence must end with . ? or ! One sentence = one punctuation mark at the end.

‚úì Commas for Clarity

Use commas to separate items, clauses, and extra information. Don't use commas to connect two complete sentences.

‚úì Semicolons & Colons

Semicolons connect ideas; colons introduce what follows. Both are stronger than commas.

‚úì Apostrophes Matter

Apostrophes show possession (Mary's) or contractions (it's). Never pluralize with an apostrophe.

‚úì Less is More

In academic writing, use periods and semicolons. Avoid excessive em dashes and exclamation marks.

‚úì Read Aloud

Read your writing aloud. Punctuation marks usually mark where you naturally pause.
